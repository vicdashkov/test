Developing Voice

1) Clear customer benefit

  • Make a task Faster and Easier with Voice

  • When choosing your voice project, start with the purpose
  • avoid taking a visual experience or an app-first experience and simply adding voice to it.

2) Great name

  • Intuitive and memorable
  • Skills with long and difficult names are likely to loose customers
  • "Alexa, ask Sleep Sounds to play rain."

3) Explain what skill can do

  • Right amount of information on the first invocation
  • On second visit pick up where you left off
  • For every interaction, keep Alexa’s responses concise so that your users stay engaged and can easily follow along.
  • One-breath test.

4) Minimizes Friction

  • Only add registration when you truly need it.

5) Surprise and Delight Customers

  • In mobile and web design, it’s important to provide a consistent customer experience

  • But with voice, it’s important to have variety.
  • build engagement over time by remembering what your users were doing last.

6) Fresh Content

  • skills that provide fresh content drive more regular usage over time.

  • house your content in a database instead of
    hardcoding it into your skill to enable fast updates.

7) Skill Is Consistently Reliable

  • Professional-grade backend for your skill.

  • response time
